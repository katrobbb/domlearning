Alrighty then! Try one of these novels:

The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society, Mary Ann Shaffer and Annie Barrows (an epistolary novel set just after World War II, as a British writer visits the members of a book club in the recently German-occupied Isle of Guernsey)

The Light in the Ruins, Chris Bohjalian (alternating between 1943 and 1955 Tuscany, this book follows a murder case tracing its origins back to the Nazi occupation of Italy)

Life After Life, Kate Atkinson (this alternative history novel follows the many lives of a woman who constantly dies and is reborn to face her inevitable destiny in WWII)

Of course! Try one of these novels:

The Girl You Left Behind, Jojo Moyes (a young widow finds herself in an ownership battle over a painting that mysteriously disappeared during the German occupation of France in World War I)

Birds Without Wings, Louis de Bernières (this award-winning romantic tragedy takes place during World War I and the decline of the Ottoman Empire in Smyrna, now Turkey)

Maisie Dobbs, Jacqueline Winspear (this mystery series features war-nurse-turned-private-investigator Maisie Dobbs in post-WWI England)

Sure we do! Try one of these novels:

Euphoria, Lily King (this love triangle set in 1930s New Guinea is loosely based on the experiences of real-life anthropologist Margaret Mead)

The Paris Wife, Paula McLain (this novel tells the fictionalized tale of Ernest Hemingway's marriage and divorce to his first wife, Hadley, in the 1920s)

The House of the Spirits, Isabel Allende (though set against the backdrop of a military coup, the story is more of a family saga with a heavy dose of magical realism common in Latin American literature)

Good choice! Try one of these novels:

The Invention of Wings, Sue Monk Kidd (a tale of slavery and the abolition movement in 1800s Charleston)

The Marriage of Opposites, Alice Hoffman (this sweeping historical novel features the family story of one of France’s greatest painters, beginning on the Carribbean island of St. Thomas in 1807)

March, Geraldine Brooks (this novel earned the Pulitzer Prize for its telling of the story of the patriarch of Little Women's March family during the American Civil War)

Good choice! Try one of these novels:

The Hundred Secret Senses, Amy Tan (a young Chinese-American girl learns about her much older sister's "previous life" in mid-19th-century southwestern China)

Things Fall Apart, Chinua Achebe (this modern classic is the first in a trilogy and features an Ibo village in Nigeria as the first European missionaries prepare to arrive at the end of the 19th century)

Snow Flower and the Secret Fan, Lisa See (about two female friends who communicate using a secret form of writing in 19th-century China, when women lived in almost total seclusion)

Good choice! Try one of these novels:

Burial Rites, Hannah Kent (a dramatic mystery inspired by the true story of a convicted murderess in 1829 Iceland)

The Luminaries, Eleanor Catton (this page-turning ghost story follows a series of mysterious events in New Zealand during the West Coast Gold Rush of the 1860s)

The Queen of the Night, Alexander Chee (the multi-layered tale of a Paris Opera star before, during and after the Franco-Prussian War at the end of the 19th century)

Sounds great! Try one of these novels set between the 15th and 17th centuries:

Poison, Sara Poole (this is the first installment in a series featuring the fictional female poisoner for the powerful Borgia family in 15th-century Rome)

Peony in Love, Lisa See (this historical love story takes the reader on a tour of both 17th-century China and the Chinese vision of the afterlife)

Girl with a Pearl Earring, Tracy Chevalier (this book imagines the life of a servant girl in the household of 17th-century Dutch painter Johannes Vermeer, who was immortalized in his famous painting Girl with a Pearl Earring)

The Middle Ages is generally considered to be between the 5th and 15th centuries. Try one of these novels set during that time period:

The Name of the Rose, Umberto Eco (this leisurely paced murder mystery features a cast of Franciscan monks in a 14th-century Italian abbey)

The White Queen, Philippa Gregory (this novel begins The Cousins' War series, which chronicles the "Wars of the Roses" between the royal families who ruled England before the Tudors)

The Pillars of the Earth, Ken Follett (if you have the time and inclination to dive into a 900-page book, this epic novel features a 12th-century monk on his quest to build "the greatest Gothic cathedral the world has ever known")

Okay, we dug waaaay back in history and found these books for you:

The Dovekeepers, Alice Hoffman (this novel chronicles the journeys of four women before the siege of Masada, Israel, just after the start of the common era in 70 AD)

The Red Tent, Anita Diamant (Biblical fiction about Dinah, a daughter of Jacob in the Old Testament)

The Secret Chord, Geraldine Brooks (this novel imagines the life of King David of Israel, approximately 1,000 years before the common era)

Wait, why are you here? Just kidding! These novels are contemporary enough not to feel like historical fiction while still exploring historical events and themes:

Girl at War, Sara Novic (the tale of a survivor and former child soldier of the Croatian War in the 1990s)

Everything I Never Told You, Celeste Ng (this novel addresses family and race issues in 1970s Ohio)

The Fishermen, Chigozie Obioma (this Biblical parable tells the tale of four brothers living under a military dictatorship in early 1990s Nigeria)
